How Tiers Work

This guide explains the tier system in plain language. The goal is simple: reward reliable attendance, keep weekly access fair, and make roster choices easy to explain.

Open queue Open season squad Open standings
Snapshot

Short version

Core First access Reliable weekly players are considered first.
Rotation Queue based Active players share the next set of spots through a queue.
Flex/Sub Remaining openings Depth players fill what is left after Core and Rotation.
Flow

How weekly access works

  1. Core players get first claim on the available roster spots.
  2. Rotation players are then ordered by fewer season appearances first.
  3. Flex/Sub fills whatever spots are still open.
  4. On matchday, the confirmed roster moves into team assignment and stat tracking.
Definitions

What each tier usually means

Core
Most reliable weekly players. They are usually in if they are available.
Rotation
Active players sharing weekly access fairly. The queue decides who is next up.
Flex/Sub
Depth players, occasional players, or newer players who fill the remaining openings.
Example

Queue example

If ten Rotation spots are open, the players with the fewest season appearances go first. A player with 3 games is ahead of a player with 5 games. If two players are tied, the app keeps them in the queue order it already has for that season.

  1. Alex has 3 games, so Alex is near the front.
  2. Ben also has 3 games, so Ben is also near the front.
  3. Chris has 4 games, so Chris is after the 3-game group.
  4. Players with 5 or 6 games wait until the lower totals are handled first.
Questions

Common questions

Does a strong player always belong in Core?

No. The app focuses on reliability and season attendance, not just playing level.

Can a player move up or down?

Yes. Pathway Planner reviews attendance evidence and recommends changes for the next season.

What is the difference between current / next-season tier and this season tier?

Current / next-season tier comes from the full squad register. This season tier is the actual tier used on the active season squad for the selected season.

Why is the queue important?

It helps Rotation players share access fairly over the full season.
