How Tiers Work

The tier system decides who plays each week. It rewards showing up consistently, keeps access fair across the squad, and makes every roster decision easy to explain.

See the flex queue Open squad Open standings
The three tiers

Every player is in one of three groups

Core Play every week You've shown up consistently over several seasons. You get a spot first whenever you're available.
Flex Take turns via queue You're active and reliable, but share spots with other flex players. The queue decides whose turn it is.
Sub Fill open spots You're newer, occasional, or building your way back. You fill whatever spots remain after Core and Flex.
Each week

How a matchday roster fills up

1. Core players claim their spots
If a core player says they're available, they're in.
2. Flex players fill in order
The queue decides who's next. Players who have played fewer games this season go first.
3. Subs take remaining spots
Whatever's left goes to sub players in order of availability.
4. Teams are assigned
Once the roster is confirmed, the app splits players into balanced teams and tracks stats.
Moving up

How do I get to a higher tier?

The app tracks your attendance automatically and recommends tier changes based on real data. Here's what it looks at:

Recent form (last 8 matchdays)
How many of the last 8 weeks did you show up? This matters most. Consistency right now beats a great streak months ago.
Season attendance
Your total games and attendance score for the current season. More games and fewer no-shows help your case.
History across seasons
How you've shown up over past seasons. Long-term reliable players get a safety net — you won't drop to Sub from a brief dip if your history is strong.
No-shows and late cancels
These count against you. One is fine. A pattern will hold you back or push you down.

The app uses all of this to help the manager review whether you should stay, move up, or move down. The public flex queue stays focused on the live line, not on the manager review screen.

Read it fast

How recommendation reviews are used

1. Now = your active tier today
This is the tier the season is using right now.
2. Suggested next = where the data would place you at review time
It shows how the plan would look if the manager rebuilt tiers from attendance evidence between roster reviews.
3. Manager review = final call
The app explains the recommendation in plain language, but recommendations are review notes and not automatic changes.

Recommendations are review notes for the manager. They are not automatic changes to your tier and they are intentionally kept separate from the public queue page.

Rough thresholds

What does it take?

These aren't hard cutoffs — the app weighs everything together — but as a rough guide:

Sub to Flex Start showing up Play at least 1 of the last 8 weeks with a decent attendance score and no pattern of no-shows. Or: have a strong track record from past seasons (8+ games, solid history score) with no current no-shows.
Flex to Core Be consistently there Play 3+ of the last 8 weeks, zero recent no-shows, season score of 8+, and at least 4 total games this season. Long-term veterans with 15+ historical games can qualify with slightly less recent activity.
Staying put Your history protects you Long-term reliable players won't drop from a short dip. The app only suggests a downgrade when both recent form and history support it.
Example

Queue example

Say there are 10 flex spots open this week. The app lines up all available flex players by who has played the fewest games this season — fewer games means you've had fewer turns, so you go first.

Alex: 3 games this season
Near the front — has had fewer turns than most.
Ben: 3 games (tied with Alex)
Same number of games, so the app breaks the tie with season points. Fewer points = higher priority.
Chris: 5 games this season
Behind the 3-game group — Chris has had more turns already.

On the final matchday of the season, the order flips: highest season points go first, so the strongest performers get priority for the finale.

Questions

Common questions

Does being a great player guarantee Core?

No. Tier is about attendance reliability, not skill level. A talented player who only shows up every other week will be Flex, not Core.

Can I move down?

Yes. If your recent attendance drops or you accumulate no-shows, the app will recommend moving you down. It works both ways — show up more and you'll move back up.

Where can I see my recommendation?

The public flex queue page shows the live line for matchday spots. Recommendation review stays in the manager planner, but the rules on this page explain what helps you move up.

What's the difference between my "current tier" and "suggested tier"?

Your current tier is what's active right now for this season. The suggested tier is what the app recommends based on your attendance data — it shows where you're heading, not where you are today.

Do recommendations happen automatically?

No. Recommendations are suggestions. The league manager reviews them and makes the final call, usually between seasons.
